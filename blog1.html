Top 5 Indian Street Foods You Must Try

Published on July 26, 2025 | By Incredible India Team

Indian street food is a burst of flavors, colors, and aromas. From spicy snacks to sweet delights, each bite is a new experience. Here are the top 5 street foods you cannot miss.

Pani Puri

Pani Puri, also known as Golgappa or Puchka in different parts of India, is one of the most loved street snacks. These crispy, hollow puris are filled with spicy tangy water, mashed potatoes, chickpeas, and tamarind chutney. Each bite is a burst of flavor, combining spicy, sour, and sweet tastes. It is especially popular in states like Maharashtra, Gujarat, and West Bengal, where every region has its own twist on the recipe.

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish that combines spicy chickpea curry (chole) with deep-fried bread (bhature). This dish originated in Punjab but is now a favorite across the country. The chickpeas are slow-cooked with onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices, making them rich and flavorful. The soft and fluffy bhature, often served with pickles and sliced onions, complements the spicy chole perfectly. It‚Äôs best enjoyed hot, with a squeeze of lemon.

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular street food that originated in Mumbai. It consists of a thick vegetable curry made from mashed potatoes, peas, capsicum, tomatoes, and a special blend of spices, all cooked with butter. This curry (bhaji) is served with soft bread rolls (pav) that are toasted with butter on a hot griddle. The dish is garnished with chopped onions, fresh coriander, and a slice of lemon, making it a hearty and satisfying meal for any time of the day.

Jalebi

Jalebi is one of the most iconic Indian sweets. It is made by deep-frying a fermented batter in spiral shapes and soaking it in saffron-infused sugar syrup. Jalebi is crispy on the outside, soft inside, and has a delightful sugary crunch with every bite. It is often enjoyed with milk or rabri (sweet condensed milk) and is a common treat during festivals and celebrations across India.

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy crepe that hails from South India. It is made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). Dosas are typically served with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar (a tangy lentil soup). Variations like Masala Dosa, filled with spicy potato stuffing, are popular across the country. Light, healthy, and full of flavor, dosa is a perfect breakfast or snack option.
