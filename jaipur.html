Discover Jaipur

About Jaipur

Jaipur, the “Pink City” of India, is a magnificent blend of royal heritage, desert charm, and architectural splendor. As the capital of Rajasthan, Jaipur is famous for its palaces, forts, bustling bazaars, and streets painted in warm pink hues.

The city’s crown jewels include the Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, and City Palace, each echoing stories of kings and warriors. Walk through vibrant markets filled with handcrafted jewelry, textiles, and traditional Rajasthani art. Savor authentic dishes like dal baati churma and ghevar while soaking in the grandeur of Rajput culture.

Jaipur isn't just a destination—it's an immersive experience in color, culture, and history that captures the essence of royal India.

Top Attractions

  • Amber Fort – A majestic hilltop fort with mirrored halls, courtyards, and elephant rides.
  • Hawa Mahal – The iconic “Palace of Winds” with 953 windows for royal ladies to observe street life.
  • City Palace – A blend of Mughal and Rajasthani architecture, still home to Jaipur’s royal family.
  • Jantar Mantar – An 18th-century astronomical observatory with massive sundials and instruments.
  • Albert Hall Museum – Rajasthan’s oldest museum showcasing royal artifacts, art, and culture.
  • Chokhi Dhani – A recreated Rajasthani village offering folk performances, food, and camel rides.
  • Bapu & Johari Bazaar – Colorful markets known for handicrafts, gemstones, and ethnic wear.

Best Time to Visit

October to March is the best time to explore Jaipur due to its pleasant weather.
