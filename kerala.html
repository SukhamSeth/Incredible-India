Discover Kerala

About Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as “God’s Own Country,” is a lush tropical state in southern India known for its serene backwaters, palm-lined beaches, rich biodiversity, and classical Indian traditions. Nestled between the Western Ghats and the Arabian Sea, Kerala offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and wellness tourism.

The tranquil backwaters of Alleppey and Kumarakom offer houseboat cruises through peaceful canals and villages. In contrast, hill stations like Munnar and Wayanad are covered in tea plantations, misty hills, and spice gardens. Kerala is also famous for its ancient Ayurvedic practices, Kathakali dance performances, and temple festivals.

Whether you're relaxing on the beach, cruising through coconut lagoons, or experiencing traditional healing, Kerala promises a peaceful, rejuvenating, and soulful journey.

Top Attractions

  • Alleppey Backwaters – Glide through palm-fringed canals and villages on a traditional houseboat.
  • Munnar Tea Gardens – Rolling hills blanketed with tea plantations, perfect for nature walks and photography.
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary – A lush forest reserve with elephants, boating, and bamboo rafting experiences.
  • Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary – A haven for migratory birds nestled in the backwaters region.
  • Kovalam & Varkala Beaches – Pristine coastal escapes ideal for swimming, surfing, and Ayurveda therapy.
  • Padmanabhaswamy Temple – A majestic and historic temple with intricate Dravidian architecture.
  • Traditional Kathakali Shows – Experience Kerala’s ancient storytelling art form through dramatic dance and music.

Best Time to Visit

October to February is the ideal time for pleasant weather and exploring the backwaters.
